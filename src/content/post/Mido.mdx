For almost three years, the Mido Ocean Star Tribute Gradient has been on my wrist every single day.

It wasn't even the plan.

Mido isn't sold in India, so I asked a friend visiting Dubai to pick one up for me. The original choice was the 38mm Hamilton Murph — the one from Interstellar. Clean dial, strong story, easy decision. Except it was sold out. The Mido was the backup.

Looking back, that turned out to be exactly the right call.

The 39mm case sits perfectly on my wrist. Not too large, not trying too hard. It has this rare quality of feeling vintage and modern at the same time — the gradient dial shifts subtly depending on the light, and the overall proportions just work. I've worn it through long motorcycle rides, slow weekends, and entirely forgettable Tuesday mornings. It never feels out of place.

I also own a Tissot Seastar Powermatic 80. A fine watch. But when I reach for a diver, it's almost always the Mido.

Its character is unhurried, unpretentious. It doesn't demand attention. It just does its job, quietly, every day.

Sometimes the watch you settle for ends up being the one you'd choose all over again.
