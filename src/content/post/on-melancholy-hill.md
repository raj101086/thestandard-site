I can't remember where I first heard On Melancholy Hill. Maybe a film. Maybe a TV series. What I do remember is pulling out Shazam immediately because I needed to know what that song was.

That was my introduction to Gorillaz and, by extension, Damon Albarn.

Gorillaz are strange in the best way. Across Demon Days, Plastic Beach, Humanz — each record sounds like a different band, yet you always know it's them. Albarn has never seemed interested in repeating himself, which makes On Melancholy Hill even more interesting. It's probably their most straightforward song. Gentle, warm, almost simple. And it's the one that got me.

It's been on every playlist I've had since. Happy days, bad ones, long drives, late nights. It fits all of them without trying to.

There's one couplet that's stuck with me for years — "you can't get what you want, but you can get me." Simple, sincere, and somehow more comforting than it has any right to be. The rest of the song earns those lines.

Fifteen years later, the song still feels like drifting through a dream at sunset.
