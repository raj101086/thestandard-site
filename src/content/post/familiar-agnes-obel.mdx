I first heard *Familiar* while watching *Dark* on Netflix.

There's a scene in the first season where everything the show has been building quietly clicks into place. The music doesn't announce it. It just arrives, and suddenly the scene is something else entirely.

I had Shazam open before it finished.

Agnes Obel's voice sits just slightly off from where you expect it. Not quite centered, not quite stable. That displacement is the whole trick. Paired with the string arrangement, it creates something that feels both very old and quietly unsettling.

Some songs feel cinematic. This one feels like fog, silence and late-night thoughts.
