I just finished *Hyperion* by Dan Simmons and I'm honestly wondering how it escaped me for so many years.

A strange, beautiful, unsettling book. Vast futuristic worlds, a constant sense of impending doom, sharp wit, poetry, religion, mystery, and some of the most imaginative science fiction I've ever read.

Every story within the pilgrimage feels unique, yet together they build something much bigger.

One line in particular has stayed with me:

> "You have to live to really know things, my love."

It somehow captures the spirit of the book itself — its curiosity, wisdom, and humanity amid all the grandeur and chaos.

It's rare to find a novel that feels both epic and deeply personal. An easy recommendation for anyone who loves science fiction.

I've already started *The Fall of Hyperion* and can't wait to see where the Cantos goes next.
